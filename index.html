B.Tech Programs in CSE, AI & ML, Data Analytics, and IT
Our B.Tech programs are designed to prepare students for the modern tech industry through innovation-driven learning, coding excellence, and hands-on projects.
  • B.Tech in Computer Science & Engineering (CSE): Core programming, Data Structures & Algorithms, Operating Systems, and Software Development.
  • B.Tech in Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning (AI & ML): Deep Learning, Neural Networks, Natural Language Processing, and AI Applications.
  • B.Tech in Data Analytics (DA): Data Science, Big Data Technologies, Statistical Analysis, and Predictive Modeling.
  • B.Tech in Information Technology (IT): Web Development, Cloud Computing, Cybersecurity, and Database Management Systems.
Students gain exposure through live projects, coding competitions, research initiatives, and internships with industry partners — ensuring both technical depth and professional readiness.
Engineering Projects — Mini, Minor, and Major
Our engineering curriculum integrates practical learning through a structured project-based approach. Students work on **mini, minor, and major projects** that build creativity, teamwork, and technical expertise.
  • Mini Projects (1st & 2nd Year): Focused on foundational concepts — simple web apps, C/Python utilities, Arduino and IoT experiments, and data visualization dashboards.
  • Minor Projects (3rd Year): Based on real-world problem statements using advanced tools — Machine Learning models, Database Management Systems, or API-based applications.
  • Major Projects (Final Year): Comprehensive industry-level or research-driven projects — Artificial Intelligence, Data Analytics platforms, Cloud-based systems, or full-stack web solutions.
Students are encouraged to publish research papers, participate in hackathons, and collaborate with startups to enhance innovation and employability.
Core & Advanced Subjects in Engineering
Our curriculum is designed to provide students with a balanced mix of theoretical knowledge and hands-on technical skills across multiple domains of Computer Science and Engineering.
  • OOPM (Object Oriented Programming Methodology): Concepts of OOP using C++ and Java, encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism.
  • DBMS (Database Management System): SQL, normalization, indexing, and relational database design with MySQL and Oracle.
  • Data Analytics: Data preprocessing, visualization, and analytical modeling using Python and statistical tools.
  • Operating System: Process scheduling, memory management, file systems, and synchronization techniques.
  • Machine Learning: Supervised and unsupervised algorithms, model training, evaluation, and deployment.
  • Introduction to Data Science: Data collection, cleaning, analysis, and storytelling with data-driven insights.
  • Image Processing: Digital image enhancement, filtering, segmentation, and computer vision basics.
  • Internet of Things (IoT): Sensor networks, embedded systems, cloud connectivity, and real-world IoT applications.
  • Data Structures with C and C++: Arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs, and algorithmic complexity.
  • Software Engineering: Software development life cycle (SDLC), agile methodologies, and software design principles.
  • Project Management: Planning, scheduling, risk management, and teamwork for technical projects.
  • Internet and Web Engineering: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, and full-stack web application development.
Each subject emphasizes practical learning through lab sessions, coding exercises, and mini-projects — helping students build a solid foundation for advanced research and industry applications.
